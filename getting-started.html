Getting Started

An overview of the DWCE Time Tracker app, how GPS is used to help you, and how your data is protected.

What is DWCE Time Tracker?

DWCE Time Tracker is the company's mobile app for recording everything that happens on site and in the yard. It replaces paper timesheets, handwritten check sheets and delivery dockets with a single, easy-to-use app on your phone.

As a site worker, you will use the app to:

  • Clock in and out at the start and end of your working day
  • Submit your daily timesheet — the hours you worked, the project or vehicle you were on, any allowances, and equipment used
  • Complete pre-start plant checks before operating large equipment
  • Check small plant and tools at your location and report any faults
  • Record aggregate and waste deliveries
  • Read messages from your supervisor or the office
✅

Everything you submit goes straight to the office in real time — no need to hand in paper at the end of the week.

What you will need

  • An Android or iOS smartphone
  • Your login credentials — provided by your employer
  • Location (GPS) permissions enabled for the app — required for clocking and site-based features
  • An internet connection (mobile data or Wi-Fi) to submit records

How GPS Works in the App

DWCE Time Tracker uses your phone's GPS in specific, clearly defined ways to make the app easier to use and to verify site attendance. The app does not track your location continuously or outside of the actions described below.

When your location is used

1. Clocking In & Out

When you clock in or out, your location at that moment is recorded. This confirms you were on or near the work site when you started and finished your shift. The location is attached to your clocking record and is visible to your supervisor.

2. Find Nearest — Timesheet Project / Fleet

When adding a time period to your timesheet, tapping the Find Nearest button uses your current GPS position to automatically suggest the closest project or fleet item to where you are standing. This saves you time searching through a long list.

3. Find Last — Timesheet Project / Fleet

The Find Last button recalls the project or fleet item you selected most recently. Your location is not used for this function — it simply saves you re-selecting the same item if you are on the same job as yesterday.

4. Plant Location

Site-based features such as plant checks and asset checks may use your location to confirm which site or area you are working in.

ℹ️

Your location is only read at the moment you perform one of the actions above. The app does not run in the background tracking your movements throughout the day.

Granting location permission

The first time you use a location-based feature, your phone will ask you to allow the app to access your location. You should select "While using the app" or "Allow". Without this permission the clocking and Find Nearest features will not work.

⚠️

If you previously denied location permission, go to your phone's Settings → Apps → DWCE Time Tracker → Permissions → Location and enable it.

GDPR & Your Privacy

DWCE is committed to handling your personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 as enacted in Ireland. This section explains what data the app collects, why, and your rights.

What data is collected

  • Identity data: Your name and work email address
  • Attendance data: Clock-in and clock-out times and your GPS location at those moments
  • Timesheet data: Hours worked, project or vehicle allocated, equipment used, allowances claimed, and any comments you add
  • Inspection data: Results of plant pre-start checks and asset checks you complete, including any fault photos you take
  • Delivery data: Details of aggregate and waste deliveries you record, including your captured signature

Why we collect it

  • To calculate your pay accurately (timesheet and attendance data)
  • To meet legal obligations for health and safety record-keeping (plant checks)
  • To fulfil contractual obligations with clients (delivery records)
  • To manage company assets and equipment effectively

Legal basis

The lawful basis for processing this data is the performance of your employment contract (Article 6(1)(b) EU GDPR). Some health and safety records are also processed to comply with a legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c) EU GDPR).

How long your data is kept

Employment and payroll records are retained for a minimum of 6 years following the end of your employment, in line with Revenue Commissioners requirements. Health and safety records may be retained for longer periods where required by law.

Who can see your data

  • Your direct supervisor and site manager (attendance, timesheets, checks)
  • The payroll team (timesheet and allowance data)
  • Company management (aggregated reports)
  • DWCE's secure cloud database provider — data is stored in an encrypted database and is not shared with any third party for marketing or commercial purposes

Your rights

Under EU GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access a copy of the personal data held about you
  • Correct any inaccurate data
  • Erasure of data in certain circumstances (note: some records must be kept by law)
  • Object to processing where you believe it is not justified
ℹ️

Data protection in Ireland is overseen by the Data Protection Commission (DPC). For more information visit dataprotection.ie.

✅

To exercise any of your data rights, contact your supervisor or the DWCE office. All requests are handled within 30 days.
