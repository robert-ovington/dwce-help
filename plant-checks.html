Plant Checks

Plant checks ensure equipment is safe and fit for use before and during operation. This section covers pre-start checks for large plant, asset checks for small plant, and how to report faults.

Large Plant

Large plant refers to heavy machinery and vehicles — excavators, dumpers, rollers, lorries, and similar equipment. Before operating any large plant, a pre-start check must be completed. The first time you use a particular machine in the app, you will need to configure it.

a. Configuring for First Time Use

Before you can complete a pre-start check for a machine, it must be set up in the app. This only needs to be done once per machine.

  1. From the main menu, tap Plant Checks then Large Plant.
  2. If the machine you want to check is not listed, tap Configure or the settings icon.
  3. Select the machine from the available list, or search for it by name or registration.
  4. Confirm the machine details shown on screen are correct (machine type, registration/ID, site location).
  5. Tap Save Configuration.
  6. The machine will now appear in your pre-start check list.
ℹ️

If the machine you need is not available in the list, contact the office — new plant items are added by an administrator.

b. Recording a Fault & Adding a Photo

If you find a defect or fault on a piece of large plant — before, during, or after a pre-start check — you must report it immediately using the fault report screen.

  1. From the Large Plant screen, select the machine with the fault.
  2. Tap Report Fault.
  3. Select the fault category from the list (e.g. engine, tyres, hydraulics, lights).
  4. Enter a description of the fault in the text field — be as specific as possible.
  5. To add a photo, tap Add Photo:
    • Choose Take Photo to use your camera directly
    • Or choose Choose from Gallery if you already have a photo saved
  6. Review the fault details and photo, then tap Submit Fault.
⚠️

Do not operate a machine with a reported safety-critical fault until it has been cleared by the site supervisor or maintenance team. Submitting a fault report in the app does not mean the machine has been cleared for use.

✅

Photos are extremely helpful for the maintenance team. Try to photograph the fault clearly, close-up, in good light. Include a wider shot showing where on the machine the fault is.

c. Submitting a Pre-Start Check

A pre-start check must be completed before you operate a piece of large plant each day. It confirms the machine is safe and fit for use.

  1. From the main menu, tap Plant Checks → Large Plant.
  2. Select the machine you are about to operate from the list.
  3. Tap New Pre-Start Check.
  4. Work through the checklist items displayed on screen. For each item, tap:
    • Pass — if the item is in good condition and working correctly
    • Fail — if the item has a defect or is not working
  5. For any item you mark as Fail, you will be prompted to add a description and optionally a photo. Complete this before continuing.
  6. Once all checklist items are answered, tap Review & Submit.
  7. Review the summary of your check — passed, failed, and any notes.
  8. Tap Submit Check to confirm.
⚠️

Do not rush through the checklist by tapping Pass on everything without physically checking each item. Pre-start checks are a legal safety requirement and must be completed honestly.

ℹ️

Your supervisor is notified automatically when a check is submitted with one or more failed items.

Small Plant

Small plant refers to hand tools, power tools, and smaller equipment — generators, compactors, vibrators, breakers, and similar items. Asset checks are carried out by location and confirm that equipment at that location is present and in acceptable condition.

a. Asset Check for a Location

An asset check is a location-based inspection. You select the site or storage area, and the app displays all items that should be present at that location.

  1. From the main menu, tap Plant Checks → Small Plant.
  2. Tap New Asset Check.
  3. Select the location you are checking from the dropdown list (e.g. a specific site, compound, or storage area).
  4. The app will display the list of items assigned to that location.
  5. For each item, indicate its status:
    • Present & OK — item is on site and in good condition
    • Present — Fault — item is on site but has a defect (you will be prompted to record the fault — see below)
    • Not Present — item is missing from the location
  6. Once all items are reviewed, tap Submit Check.
✅

Walk the location with your phone and physically check each item as you go rather than filling the form in from memory.

b. Recording a Fault & Adding a Photo

When you identify a fault on a small plant item during an asset check — or at any time — you can submit a fault report directly.

  1. From the Small Plant screen, tap Report Fault, or when completing an asset check, select Present — Fault on the relevant item.
  2. Select the equipment item with the fault (if not already selected).
  3. Select the fault type from the list.
  4. Enter a clear description of the fault.
  5. Tap Add Photo to take a photo or choose one from your gallery. A photo is strongly recommended.
  6. Tap Submit to send the fault report.
⚠️

Tag or set aside any equipment with a reported fault so colleagues know it should not be used until repaired or replaced.

My Checks

My Checks shows a history of all pre-start and asset checks you have submitted. Use it to confirm a check was recorded correctly or to review past results.

  1. From the main menu, tap Plant Checks → My Checks.
  2. A list of your submitted checks appears, ordered by date — most recent first.
  3. Tap any entry to view the full details of that check, including each checklist item result and any fault notes.

What the summary shows

  • Date and time the check was submitted
  • Machine or location checked
  • Overall result — all passed, or number of items failed
  • Individual item results and any fault descriptions or photos
ℹ️

My Checks is a read-only record. Submitted checks cannot be edited. If you submitted a check with an error, notify your supervisor.
