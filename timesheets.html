Timesheets

Your timesheet is made up of individual Time Periods — one for each block of work in a day. This section covers how to add, view, edit and understand your timesheet.

Adding a Time Period

A Time Period is a single record of work — it captures the date, the hours you worked, what project or vehicle you were on, and any additional information such as equipment used, allowances, and materials. You should add a time period for each distinct block of work in your day.

  1. From the main menu, tap Timesheets.
  2. Tap the + Add Time Period button.
  3. Work through each section of the form (described below).
  4. When all required fields are complete, tap Save to review the submission popup and confirm.

a. Date, Time & Breaks

This section sets the date and the hours worked for this time period.

Date

Tap the Date field to open the date picker. By default it will be set to today's date. Scroll to select a different date if you are entering a time period for a previous day.

⚠️

Always check the date is correct before saving. Timesheets submitted for the wrong date will need to be corrected by your supervisor.

Start Time & End Time

Tap the Start Time field and use the time picker to set the time you began working. Repeat for End Time when you finished.

The app automatically calculates your total hours based on the start and end times minus any breaks you record.

Breaks

If you took an unpaid break during this time period, record it in the Break field. Enter the total duration of unpaid breaks in minutes. This amount is deducted from your total working hours.

✅

Only enter breaks that were unpaid. If you had a paid lunch break, do not include it here.

b. Project / Fleet

This section links your time period to either a Project (a specific job or contract) or a Fleet item (a vehicle or piece of plant). You will switch between the two depending on the nature of your work.

Switching between Project and Fleet

At the top of this section you will see two tabs or a toggle: Project and Fleet. Tap the one that applies to your work:

  • Project — use this if your time is allocated to a specific site or contract
  • Fleet — use this if your time is allocated to a specific vehicle or machine

Searching and filtering the list

If there are many projects or fleet items, use the Filter / Search bar to type part of the name and narrow down the list quickly.

Find Nearest

Tap Find Nearest to use your current GPS location to automatically identify and select the closest project or fleet item to where you are standing. This is the quickest option when you are already on site.

Find Last

Tap Find Last to instantly re-select the project or fleet item you used in your most recent time period. Use this if you are on the same job as your last entry and want to save time scrolling or searching.

✅

Find Last does not use GPS — it simply recalls your previous selection. It works offline too.

c. Fleet & Equipment

In addition to your primary project or fleet allocation, you can record any specific vehicles or equipment you operated or used during this time period.

Adding equipment

  1. In the Fleet & Equipment section, tap + Add.
  2. Select the equipment from the list, or use the search bar to find it.
  3. The item will be added to your time period.

Adding multiple items

You can add more than one piece of equipment. Repeat the steps above for each item you used during this time period.

Removing an item

To remove an equipment item you have added by mistake, tap the remove (×) icon next to the item in the list.

Saving your fleet selection

Your equipment selection is saved as part of the time period when you tap the main Save button at the end of the form. There is no need to save this section separately.

d. Allowances

Allowances are additional payments beyond your basic hourly rate. The app provides two types: Miscellaneous and Travel.

Miscellaneous Allowances

Miscellaneous allowances cover site-specific entitlements such as a site allowance, meal allowance, or other agreed payments. The allowances available to you are pre-configured by the office based on the project or site you are working on.

  • Tap the Miscellaneous Allowances section to expand it
  • Enable the allowances that apply to your work on this day
  • The value of each allowance is set by the office — you select whether it applies, not the amount

Travel Allowance

Travel allowance compensates you for travelling to a site. The calculation is based on rules configured by the office (for example, a rate per mile or a flat daily rate depending on distance).

  • Enable the Travel Allowance toggle if it applies to this time period
  • The app will calculate the allowance amount based on the project location and your configured rate
  • The calculated amount will be shown for your reference before you save
ℹ️

If you believe an allowance is showing the wrong amount, or an allowance you are entitled to is not listed, contact the office — allowance configuration is managed by administration.

e. Materials (Concrete Lorry Drivers only)

The Materials section is only visible to drivers of the concrete mixing lorry. It allows you to record the materials loaded and delivered during this time period as part of your work record.

🔹

If you do not see a Materials section on your timesheet form, this feature has not been enabled for your account. This is expected — it is a driver-specific feature.

Recording materials

  1. Tap + Add Material.
  2. Select the material type from the list.
  3. Enter the quantity.
  4. Tap Add to save the entry.
  5. Repeat for each material loaded during this time period.

f. Comments

The Comments field is a free-text area where you can add any relevant notes about this time period. This is optional but can be useful for recording context that is not captured elsewhere in the form.

Examples of useful comments

  • "Concrete pour on foundation — worked alongside groundworks crew"
  • "Plant breakdown from 10:00 to 12:00 — waiting on repairs"
  • "Assisted delivery driver in afternoon"
  • "Site access delayed — started 45 minutes late due to gate issue"
✅

Comments are visible to your supervisor when reviewing timesheets. Keep them factual and professional.

g. Reviewing Before Saving

Before your time period is saved to the system, the app displays a submission summary popup. This is your opportunity to check all the details are correct before confirming.

What the popup shows

  • Date and day of the week
  • Start time, end time, and total hours worked
  • Break duration
  • Project or fleet item selected
  • Equipment items added
  • Allowances applied and their values
  • Materials (if applicable)
  • Your comment (if entered)

Confirming or going back

  • If everything is correct, tap Confirm & Save to submit the time period
  • If something is wrong, tap Go Back (or close the popup) to return to the form and make corrections
⚠️

Always review the summary carefully. Once saved, a time period can still be edited, but only within a set window of time. After that window closes, corrections require supervisor approval.

My Timesheet — Viewing, Editing & Deleting

My Timesheet shows all the time periods you have submitted, organised by date. Use it to check what you have entered, make corrections, or remove incorrect entries.

  1. From the main menu, tap My Timesheet.
  2. Your list of submitted time periods will appear, most recent at the top.
  3. Tap any time period to open its full details.

Viewing a Time Period

Tapping a time period opens a read-only summary of everything recorded for that entry — date, hours, project/fleet, equipment, allowances, and comments.

Editing a Time Period

If you made a mistake on a saved time period, you can edit it while it is still within the editable window (your employer sets this window — typically the same day or within a few days).

  1. Open the time period you want to edit.
  2. Tap the Edit button (pencil icon).
  3. Make the necessary changes to any field.
  4. Tap Save and confirm in the popup.
ℹ️

If the Edit button is greyed out or not visible, the editable window for that time period has closed. Contact your supervisor to request an amendment.

Deleting a Time Period

If you have added a time period in error (for example, a duplicate entry), you can delete it while it is within the editable window.

  1. Open the time period you want to delete.
  2. Tap the Delete button (bin icon).
  3. A confirmation prompt will appear — tap Confirm to delete permanently.
⚠️

Deletion cannot be undone. Only delete a time period if you are certain it was entered in error.

My Hours

My Hours gives you a simple summary of the hours recorded across your submitted time periods. Use it to check your running total for the week or pay period.

  1. From the main menu, tap My Hours.
  2. The screen displays a summary of your submitted time data grouped by week or pay period.

What My Hours shows

  • Total hours submitted for the current period
  • A breakdown of hours by day
  • The status of your submission (submitted / pending supervisor approval)
✅

My Hours shows the data you have entered in the app. If a time period is still pending approval it may not yet be reflected in your payroll — check with the office if you have queries about your pay.
